Tuesdays at Six by Lilli North

A second-chance, high-heat rural romance

Emma Jacobs never expected to return to her family’s horse farm in northern England. Drawn home by grief, she reconnects with Luke Carter, her childhood best friend and first love. Bound by a weekly Tuesday ritual, they navigate loss, memory, and longing in a story of second chances, slow-burn intimacy, and small-town ties that refuses to let go.

Analysis Overview

Readership
The readership is anchored by women aged 25–44 who actively engage in BookTok, Bookstagram, and audiobook platforms. They favour emotionally authentic romances that blend grief and healing with high-heat intimacy, especially within small-town or rural settings. Secondary audiences from women’s fiction and mature drama add further breadth, discovering the book through libraries, drama tie-ins, and traditional outlets.
Classification
Using a bespoke sub-genre taxonomy, the work positions itself as a high-heat second-chance contemporary romance that intersects with small-town life, grief-and-healing arcs, and friends-to-lovers tropes, filling a gap in UK-set rural romance.
Personas
The primary persona, Sarah the Nostalgic Romantic, embodies the heart of the readership: digitally active, socially connected, and seeking affirmation of enduring love. The secondary persona, David the Reflective Widower, highlights male crossover interest and a desire for emotionally resonant stories of renewal.
Marketing Guidance
Marketing insights provide clear direction for early-stage campaigns, suggesting creative hooks rooted in ritual, grief-to-love arcs, and rural texture. These can shape copy, video teasers, and book club positioning. While currently delivered within this report, future iterations will expand into a standalone marketing toolkit.

Marketing Strategy

Pre-Launch

6 weeks pre-launch (awareness)

  • Introduce core themes of love, grief, and belonging via broad social media and digital ads.
  • Short-form video teasers, quote graphics, boosted posts
  • A/B test creatives against emotions of nostalgia and desire.

Launch

2 weeks around launch (conversion)

  • Deepen interest through author features, comparative hooks, and reader testimonials.
  • Carousel ads, Instagram stories, podcast guesting
  • Segment by audience (romance readers, book clubs, mature audiences).

Post-Launch

6 weeks post-launch (sustain momentum)

  • Drive pre-orders and book club selections via email, retargeting, and partnerships.
  • Discount offers, exclusive chapters, early reviews
  • Retarget engaged users with urgency-based messaging.
