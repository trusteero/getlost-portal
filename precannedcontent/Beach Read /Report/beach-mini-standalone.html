Beach Read by Emily Henry

A heartfelt contemporary romance about grief, healing and unexpected love by a summer lake.

January Andrews, reeling from grief and creative block, escapes to her late father’s lakeside cottage where she collides with her brooding neighbour Gus. As two writers confronting loss, identity, and buried emotion, they discover a connection that slowly deepens into love. The novel blends humour, emotional introspection, and romantic tension into a moving, character-driven story.

Audience Insights

BISAC Categories

Primary: Contemporary Romance

Secondary: Women's Fiction

Additional: Upmarket Fiction; Grief & Sudden Loss Fiction

Key Characteristics

The core readership consists of predominantly female adults aged 25–44 who seek emotionally resonant, character-driven fiction exploring grief, healing and intimate relationships. They favour romance with depth, authenticity and psychological nuance.

Analysis Overview

Classification

The book sits at the intersection of contemporary romance and upmarket women's fiction, offering emotional realism, grief exploration and literary-leaning introspection. It fits comfortably within small-town summer romances with psychological depth and occupies a competitive space alongside titles by Emily Henry, Christina Lauren, and Sally Rooney, distinguished by its bittersweet tone and authentic portrayal of creative and personal recovery.

Personas

Two clear personas emerge: Amelia, a creative, introspective millennial seeking emotional catharsis and authenticity; and Daniel, a mature, emotionally curious reader drawn to character-driven fiction that expands his emotional vocabulary. Both connect deeply with themes of grief, transformation, and slow-building romantic tension.

Marketing Guidance

Marketing should emphasise emotional healing, lakeside intimacy and a blend of humour and heartache. Position it as a summer read for fans of emotionally intelligent romance, using BookTok aesthetics, influencer-driven campaigns and strong character-led hooks. Emotional storytelling, grief-to-healing arcs and upmarket packaging will maximise reach across romance, women’s fiction and book club channels.

Mood & Tone

Bittersweet

Grief and healing run beneath the romantic arc, shaping the emotional texture and giving the narrative depth.

Reflective

The first-person voice blends introspection with humour as January processes loss, identity and creative block.

Romantic Tension

Slow-building chemistry between January and Gus creates emotional and narrative drive, balancing humour and vulnerability.

Hopeful

Despite the weight of grief, the story moves toward reconnection, growth and a believable, emotionally grounded future.
